Research Overview

Why Neuro­marketing Needs XAI

Consumer decision-making is shaped by subconscious neural processes that traditional self-report methods cannot access. By combining EEG brain signals with observable behavioral patterns — eye-tracking, reaction times, gaze sequences — we can build far richer predictive models.

But prediction alone is not enough. Our research goes further: we apply Explainable AI (XAI) to make these models transparent, interpretable, and ethically aligned — ensuring insights are trustworthy and actionable for marketers, researchers, and regulators alike.

This four-component framework covers multimodal fusion, temporal behavioral dynamics, ethical transparency, and adaptive context-aware prediction — forming a complete end-to-end system for next-generation consumer intelligence.

Four Research Components

Each Layer of the Framework

Our research is structured across four complementary components, each tackling a distinct dimension of the consumer decision-making problem — from raw signal processing to ethical accountability.

COMPONENT 01
Neural + Behavioral Fusion

Multimodal Neural–Behavioral Integration

Synchronized EEG and behavioral datasets are acquired, preprocessed, and fused through an attention-based deep learning architecture. Unimodal baselines (CNN-LSTM for EEG, LSTM/GRU for behavior) are compared against the joint multimodal model. SHAP values reveal which neural and behavioral features drive consumer choices.

EEG (OpenNeuro, DEAP) CNN-LSTM Attention Fusion SHAP ICA Artifact Removal

COMPONENT 02
Temporal Dynamics

Behavioral Dynamics in Decision-Making

How do decisions evolve moment-to-moment? This component models the temporal structure of consumer behavior using LSTM and GRU sequence models. Unsupervised clustering uncovers latent decision styles — impulsive, reflective, vacillating — and PsychoPy-controlled experiments capture fine-grained behavioral signals across value trade-off scenarios.

COMPONENT 03
Ethical Transparency

Ethically Transparent XAI Framework

Predictive power without transparency raises ethical red flags. This component wraps the predictive models in a four-module XAI layer: dual-layer explainability (neural time-series + behavioral psychology), counterfactual "what-if" scenarios, ethical scoreboards measuring trust and manipulation risk, and interactive dashboards for non-technical stakeholders.

SHAP / LIME Counterfactuals (DiCE) Fairlearn / AIF360 Trust Index Bias Detection

COMPONENT 04
Adaptive AI

Contextual Decision Modelling & Adaptive AI

Real-world consumers don't behave like lab subjects. This component introduces a context-aware layer that conditions predictions on product category, device, emotional state, and environment. Meta-learning enables rapid personalization for new users, while contextual bandit policies select optimal content and nudges from continuous interaction feedback.

System Pipeline

From Raw Signal
to Actionable Insight

01
Data Acquisition

EEG signals are sourced from public repositories (OpenNeuro, DEAP) and collected from volunteer participants in controlled experiments. Behavioral data — eye-tracking metrics, reaction times, gaze sequences, and self-reported difficulty — is captured using PsychoPy-designed decision scenarios involving value trade-offs.

OpenNeuro DEAP Dataset MAMEM PsychoPy
02
Preprocessing & Feature Engineering

EEG signals undergo bandpass filtering, ICA-based artifact removal, and frequency-band extraction (alpha, beta, theta). Behavioral streams are cleaned, synchronized, and normalized. EEG epochs are converted to time-frequency representations; behavioral sequences are encoded into temporal feature sets ready for modeling.

MNE Python ICA Bandpass Filter Fixation Detection Temporal Alignment
03
Unimodal Baselines & Behavioral Clustering

CNN-LSTM models capture spatial-temporal EEG patterns; LSTM/GRU models handle behavioral sequences. Simultaneously, unsupervised temporal clustering (TASC and motif-based) segments participants into latent decision strategy groups — enabling a richer understanding before multimodal fusion.

CNN-LSTM LSTM / GRU K-Means
04
Multimodal Attention-Based Fusion

EEG and behavioral representations are fused through cross-modal attention mechanisms. A context-aware modulation layer (FiLM/hypernetwork) conditions the fused representation on situational variables. Meta-learned user adapters enable fast personalization, and a contextual bandit policy selects optimal actions from continuous feedback.

Cross-Attention FiLM Modulation MAML Contextual Bandit Transformer
05
XAI Layer & Ethical Evaluation

SHAP values and integrated gradients attribute neural and behavioral contributions to each prediction. Counterfactual explanations generate actionable "what-if" scenarios. Ethical scoreboards quantify trust indices, bias across demographic groups, and manipulation risk — providing auditable accountability for every prediction.

SHAP LIME DiCE Counterfactuals Fairlearn AIF360
Explainability & Ethics

AI That Explains Itself

Black-box predictions erode trust. Our XAI framework ensures every consumer insight comes with a clear, auditable explanation — accessible to researchers, marketers, and regulators equally.

🧠

Dual-Layer Explainability

Neural time-series attribution highlights which EEG moments influence decisions. Behavioral attribution maps model outputs to established cognitive psychology principles.

🔀

Counterfactual "What-If" Scenarios

Interactive exploration of how small changes in neural or behavioral inputs would alter predicted outcomes — actionable and non-manipulative by design.

⚖️

Ethical Scoreboards

Quantified trust indices, bias detection across demographic groups, and manipulation risk scores give every prediction a measurable ethical profile.

📊

Adaptive Temporal Saliency

For sequential decisions, attention visualization and temporal saliency maps pinpoint the exact moments when behavioral shifts occur and explain why.
